What is climate change and pollution?

Defined by NASA, climate change is the change in our global and local climates overtime. The main things that contribute to this is the burning of fossil feuls, which increase the number of heat-trapping greenhouse gasses. According to National Geographic, pollution is the introduction of harmful chemicals and substances into our environment. This includes plastic, foam, and oil.

Environmental Racism

The Century Foundation says environmental racism is a systemic issue that results in people of color being segregated against when buying houses. This happens because POC, specifically Black Americans, are often centered in financially disempowered areas near landfills and high-pollution sites. Since these properties are low priced, it attracts low-income people, preventing them from building wealth and creating a never-ending cycle.

Food Waste

According to the NRDC, 40% of food is wasted. With food being wasted, the carbon emissions are still being produced and emitted into the atmospehere. Even in landfills, wasted food creates methane, a very powerful CO2 gas.

The Bottom Line

Climate change is threatening our future of the world. There is no certianty as to what will happen. However, we can use what is happening right now as a gauge for what will happen in the future. Animal extinction, the icecaps melting, and the coral reef deterioration are some of the current effects of climate change. These deeply effect our lifestyle and economy. Good news- we can do something. We have the means and capability for impact.
